Organizational Structure

General Assembly

Consists of certified sign language interpreters who have passed the professional practice exam.

Association Regulations

  • Voting is limited to the administrative board.
  • No agreement may be concluded without the approval of the board of directors.
  • Membership may be revoked with stated reasons.
  • Annual membership fees are determined by the administrative board.
  • The executive council is elected by the administrative board.

Membership Requirements

  • Commitment to paying membership fees.
  • Not acting against the goals of the association.
  • Not causing confusion or violating translation standards.

Executive Council and Committees

Composed of elected members, each supervising a specialized committee:

1. Scientific (Academic) Committee

  • Conducting studies and research related to sign language.
  • Coordinating with the Ministries of Education and Higher Education to develop education for the deaf.
  • Setting regulations for interpreters working in the academic field.

2. Training Committee

  • Organizing conferences and workshops for interpreters.
  • Monitoring ongoing training and applying international standards.
  • Coordinating with institutions to develop interpreters' skills.

3. Legal and Media Committee

  • Defending the rights of the deaf and interpreters.
  • Advocating for official recognition of sign language.
  • Ensuring the participation of the deaf in elections using sign language.

4. Social (Cultural) Committee

  • Organizing meetings and events to promote sign language culture.
  • Communicating with interpreters and solving their issues.
  • Organizing social and recreational activities.

5. Organizational Committee

  • Following up on association affairs with government entities and completing transactions.
