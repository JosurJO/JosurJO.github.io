Jordanian Sign Language Interpreters Association

Origin of the Association

The idea of establishing the association began in early 2014, initiated by a group of interpreters working in this field and residing in Jordan, led by Mr. Osama Al-Tahrawi.

This is considered the first professional association of its kind in the Arab world. Its members are certified sign language interpreters. It is a professional, not a charitable, organization.

About the Association

The idea to establish the first Jordanian Association for Sign Language Interpreters began in early 2014 by a group of specialized interpreters led by Mr. Osama Al-Tahrawi. Official licensing was obtained by the end of the same year.

The association is a non-profit professional organization, the first of its kind in the Arab world, and includes officially certified sign language interpreters as members.

Association Goals

  • Establishing a general professional framework for sign language interpreters regarding their rights and duties, and promoting it through related institutions via official correspondence, media, etc.
  • Establishing and applying national standards for the profession, making these available to individuals and institutions, and clarifying the interpreter’s role.
  • Developing and enhancing interpreting skills, and offering continuous training programs based on best quality standards.
  • Partnering with institutions such as deaf clubs, associations, schools, and universities to discuss and develop issues related to sign language.
  • Exchanging information and maintaining communication with relevant entities to ensure accurate application of international quality standards.
  • Encouraging projects that improve services for the deaf in the Kingdom and supporting national institutions focused on interpreters and the deaf community.
  • Creating a database of sign language interpreters in Jordan and sharing it with relevant institutions.
  • Adopting practices of high-efficiency global organizations to improve service quality and align with international standards.
